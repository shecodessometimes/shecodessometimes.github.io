I never really looked into math modelling at all prior to junior year, but our math teacher, Mr. Regele, introduced us to HiMCM, and I guess it all went downhill from there.

HiMCM

HiMCM is a COMAP math modelling competition that all juniors take part in. It is a 36 hour event in October, with four people per group, where students are given an ambiguous problem, and have to find a mathematical model as the solution. My team, Garima, Rumaisa, Izzy, and I, chose to model the first of the two problems, Charge! and Bottle Battles. This is the final paper that my group submitted to HIMCM. We were designated as finalists, a distinction only given to six percent of the submitted papers.

Mathworks

The Mathworks Math Modeling Challenge is a math modelling competition from Mathworks that takes place over twelve hours. However, only two teams from our school could compete, and luckily I was on one of them. Unfortunately, my team didn't do as well in this challenge as we had in HiMCM, and our final paper was designated a successful participant, but we still had a blast. The problem centered around the electricity consumption of semi-trucks, and our final paper is shown below.

IMMC

After having finished as finalists in HiMCM, our team found that anyone who placed Meritorious or higher in HiMCM qualified for the US Regional round for the International Math Modelling Competition, and if we were one of the top two teams, we would be able to represent the US in the IMMC. However, as the time for the IMMC came around, we realized that because of the COVID-19 pandemic, we would not be able to meet each other in person. We decided that we really wanted to participate, and coordinated our efforts over zoom. The problem centered around the flood of people in flash sales and the resulting damage of store items. We ended up producing a model that relies on two main computer simulations, one a "game" in Python that simulates the amount of products sold based on where each department is placed, and the other a simulation in Java that has people objects come into the store and buy or damage items based on probability (this was the simulation that I mainly worked on). We ended up being one of the top six teams in the United States, with our paper winning a Finalist designation. Our paper is attached below.
