I've always been fascinated by poetry. I joined MAMS' Slam Poetry elective junior year, and I loved the experience. Here are some of the pieces of poetry I'm the most proud of.

she walks in the warm shadow of

streetlamps hugging

the ground beneath her giving way to

the stars above her that shine

shine so brightly

as she wanders through

those empty streets

she smiles even in the

rain that circles around

her threading through the

light because she knows

the dark will catch her even if she falls.

her wings are clipped

at least that’s what they said

she shone brilliantly once

her light illuminating

even the darkest of nights

now she’s fallen

simply fallen


her wings are clipped

at least that’s what she said

her eyes are blank

her wrists scarred

she doesn’t notice the days passing

or the seasons fading

all she can see is shadow


and yet if i could turn back time

if i had been there for her

would i tell her to fly away

or clip her wings sooner

With autumn comes a gust of breeze,

A gust that ripples through the trees,

A gust that puts the sea to shame;

Sweet and soft,

Yet a lion's mane.


Though light, this wind is not to be taken lightly,

For though its eternal dances are sightly,

Its flirtatious dance is fickle;

The rain yet to come

Is just a trickle


Whene'er that ephemeral hand touches so sweet,

I cannot help but see

Visions of what has been and what had yet to be


Flashes of anger

Flashes of light

Flashes of passion

Burning bright

The sickly sweet residue of a life so gay

It turns the dreary night to day


The wind brings memories

Soft and true

The gentle caresses

Of an ocean blue

The vicious upheavals

Of a water so rough

The overwhelming joy

Of sailing a puff


It's like

I

can't

breathe

for fear of waking

that sleeping monster

inside me


That monster that screams

Just waiting to be let out

That monster that hurls

That monster that shouts

That monster that begs of me

Please, let me stay

Please let me sail

Just one more day


When that monster roars

I cannot help but hear

That the strength of its shouting

Gives way to fear

The fear the wind readily lends

That this may truly be the end.


But even so

I have to believe

That it's not.


That one fateful day

Come whatever else may

I will be back on the water

And when I see

Such a magnificent gust of wind

I will release my monster

And let the puff take me in.
