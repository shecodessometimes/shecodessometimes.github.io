Here are some photos I took for my Landscape Photography class in Chile that I really like. I wanted the photos to have a sort of waxy, dreamlike quality to them.

I called this project "No Pasar" (do not pass) because how many signs there were on the various trails we went on that had this written on them, in order to preserve the ecosystem of the surrounding area. In my project, I wanted that sense of nature being forbidden, something ancient and ever-present that looked at us tiny humans as trespassers in a foreign land. All the photos are edited to have a waxy reddish quality to them, adding to the sense of seeing from the eye of something that is not quite human.
