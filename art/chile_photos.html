I was fortunate enough to get to go to Chile the summer after my freshman year at Northeastern, through the Dialogue of Civilizations program. On this program, we studied bacterial samples from the Atacama desert (the closest Martian analog in the world) as well as landscape photography. I decided to center my final photography project around "No Pasar" (Spanish for "do not pass") as an ode to the many such signs the trails we went to.

In my project, I wanted that sense of nature being forbidden, something ancient and ever-present that looked at us tiny humans as trespassers in a foreign land. To that effect, all the photos are edited to have a waxy reddish quality to them, adding to the sense of seeing from the eye of something that is not quite human. The resulting project is below.
